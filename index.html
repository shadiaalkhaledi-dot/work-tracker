Color key — what each color means
Where — section headers carry the program's identity color: Concourse B · Centralized / PBS · Cross-program. Black-edged blocks = the RFI sub-system inside a package.
Whose move / state — the line under each title: terracotta = blocking · teal = on you · grey = waiting on someone / watch.
The card's left edge carries two things at once — its color is the state (terracotta = blocking · teal = open · grey dashed = watch), and its thickness is derived urgency: thick = act now (overdue / blocking / due today) · medium = dated soon · hairline = no clock. Same logic in lists: items get thick edges, dated history/timeline rows get thin ones.
Attention — amber always means "look at this": ★ HIGH impact, PENDING captures, date-TBD submissions, the Morning preset.
Action pills — colored for recognition, matching the filter chips above (model · review · coordinate · respond · chase · admin) — the word is the meaning, the color just speeds it up. Category tags stay neutral.

One item = one workstream. If a card keeps getting messy, it's usually two items — Claude proposes the split at check-in.

2 · One home, many lenses

Every item lives in exactly one place (Program → Package — the filing cabinet). Everything else is a lens — an independent dial you can filter by, cutting across all programs at once:

courtwhose move is it right now — a person or You; can be a short list (first name = the bottleneck)
then →who the ball goes to next — powers "coming to my court"
delegatestill your responsibility, they execute — stays in your accountability view
horizontoday · week · sprint (until the package's next submission) · watch (no clock)
actionthe mode of work, from the Next line's verb: model · review · coordinate · respond (you owe an answer) · chase (you want theirs) · admin
disciplinewhat trade the item is about — the subject, nothing else
categorywhat kind of thing it is: decisions · meetings · coordination · documentation · drawings · rfi. Rule: action = how you work, category = what it is — never overlap
3 · How a card earns its look

Urgency is computed, never hand-set — it becomes the edge thickness:

overdueor blockingor due ≤ 3d / today→ thick edge — act now
due within 10 days→medium edge — it thickens as the date approaches
no date→hairline — no clock

Importance (★ HIGH) is separate and rare. Claude proposes it only from a named signal — critical path · cost · safety/code · cross-package ripple · a decision others wait on · owner (YYC) visibility. No signal, no star. Urgent×important together pick your Today five, always plus 1–2 important-not-urgent items so strategic work isn't buried.

5 · "Coordinating with" — the derived lens

The Discipline filter shows what an item is about. "Coordinating with" answers a different question: whose hat is the ball under? It's never tagged — it's derived: the item's court names a person → Stakeholders.md says what hat they wear → the item lands in that group. Use it to batch conversations: before the mechanical call, open Court → "Coordinating with → Mechanical" and everything waiting on a mechanical person is one list.

item: court = Greg Pink→ Stakeholders: Greg = EL · SMP→ shows under "Coordinating with → Electrical"
